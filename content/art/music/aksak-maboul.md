## Overview

Aksak Maboul is a Belgian avant-garde music collective whose work pushes the boundaries of contemporary music through experimental composition, unconventional instrumentation, and conceptual rigor. Their body of work demonstrates a commitment to exploring music as a serious artistic medium capable of addressing complex themes through sonic means.

The collective's album *Onze Danses Pour Combattre la Migraine* (Eleven Dances to Fight Migraine) stands as a unique artistic engagement with migraine itself as the explicit subject of musical composition. Rather than treating migraine as a background condition or personal affliction, Aksak Maboul made it the central conceptual and sonic focus of an entire album. The work represents an innovative approach to the Migraine Art concept: instead of visual representation or literary narrative, the artists chose music and sound as the vehicle for expressing migraine's phenomenology.

![Aksak Maboul, Onze Danses Pour Combattre la Migraine (CD reissue)](/art/images/mus-aksak-cd__Aksak_Maboul_odpclm_CD_en.jpg)

The album's experimental character reflects the condition's own strangeness—the distortions of perception, the intensity of sensation, the psychological disruption that migraine creates. By making migraine the subject of avant-garde musical exploration, Aksak Maboul elevated it from a medical or personal concern into a territory where it could be examined, interrogated, and artistically reimagined. The eleven dances suggest both the variety of migraine's manifestations and the possibility of encountering—or even dancing with—the condition rather than simply enduring it passively.

The album has been reissued in CD format, extending its reach beyond the original vinyl release and making this distinctive artistic response to migraine available to contemporary audiences. Aksak Maboul's work demonstrates that music is not incidental to the migraine art project but a powerful medium through which the condition's lived reality can be explored and transformed into something unexpected and generative.
